Out on a walk with my dog, we came across a filing cabinet on the sidewalk. This was a particularly good find. Five drawers, smooth action, art deco hardware... Even though I already have a large filing cabinet in my office (and a small two drawer one), I figured this one was coming home with me.

However. Grey in Seattle simply will not do. I had just been to the Guggenheim during a New York trip and seen [Carol Bove's magnificent metal sculptures](https://www.guggenheim.org/exhibition/carol-bove), painted (or powder coated) wonderful bright colors.

![](IMG_4458.jpeg)

I like the yellow ones. Why not a sunny filing cabinet?

I had no real luck calling companies to paint or powder coat this for me. But [this article suggested](https://boopyprojects.wordpress.com/2010/07/01/file-cabinet-planters/) it's not too challenging to spray it yourself. Their results took one can of primer and two cans of color. 

With my friend James visiting for the week, the spray challenge was on! As we painted I decided that the objective here was not just to lay on a couple quick coats and move on. We were going to do a Good Job™️. 

![](IMG_0306.jpeg)

We quickly learned a few things:

* This was going to need more paint than estimated.
* We needed to very thoroughly mask the inside of the cabinet.
* Probably a good idea to wear gloves.

![](IMG_0315.jpeg)

A later learning: Spraying horizontally only made a stripey mess. See the bottom of the cabinet in this image. You really need to make a crosshatch pattern to get it very even. In retrospect, this could have used less paint if we had known earlier.

![](IMG_0319.jpeg)

Several afternoons in, the cabinet was done. James suggested we skip painting the drawers. When we discovered that not all of the drawer hardware could be removed, we also discussed skipping masking out the center of the card slot.

This was not *The Vision*.

![](IMG_0348.jpeg)

Masking the card slots wasn't too challenging, but masking around the edge and the sides was a little tricky. Plastic trash bags helped keep spray out of the interior. A little blue tack covered the latch nub.

![](IMG_0356.jpeg)

Getting the drawers right was much easier with the crosshatching technique we perfected on the cabinet. However, peeling off the masking gave me some trouble, and I had some small areas flake off. Here you can see these are not quite finished: Drawer 3 has one fewer coats than the others.

Corrections to uneven areas were made by wet sanding with 320 grit sandpaper gently and trying again. This worked well for small areas, as well as for evening out drips and rough spots on the cabinet sides.

![](IMG_0370.jpeg)

Finally, I printed labels for the drawer slots. These are just a nice big Futura text, a suggestion I took from [Maurice Moves' organizing video](https://youtu.be/_SNiAdmA51Q?si=Mt-ZPKWcfQdbVrY2&t=166). All my bins at home have nice big Futura labels now.

![](IMG_0371.jpeg)

Final touch, most things in my office are on wheels. The cabinet looks fantastic in its new home.

In fact, it quite literally brightens my office. Sunlight from my window reflects off it, making the room glow a wonderful sunny yellow.

![](IMG_0369.jpeg)

This project was a little more challenging than I expected, even though I was accounting for [Hofstadter's Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hofstadter%27s_law). Ultimately it took about 3 cans of primer and 10 cans of color to do the whole thing. Rough cost of about $100.

If I were doing this again, I would have shopped around more for someone to do it for me first. James suggested an automotive painter shortly after we got started, but I was pretty committed at this point. Other things I would have done differently:

* Wear gloves from the start.
* Consider overspray much more.
* Maybe read up more about masking for spray painting? The flaking and peeling was very annoying.
* Bought mineral spirits or paint thinner earlier. Very useful for cleaning up errors!

Most importantly though, it just felt *really* good to make something that matched my vision. It took several weeks to plan and three weeks to execute, but wow is it satisfying to have it in place!
